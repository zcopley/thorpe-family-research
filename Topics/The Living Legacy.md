# The Living Legacy: From the Archives to the Future

The Thorpe family research vault is more than a collection of census records and pedigree charts. It is a bridge across time, connecting the pioneers of the 18th and 19th centuries to the current and future generations. 

## The Merging of the Lines

For over 150 years, the ancestors of this family traveled separate paths. The **[[Topics/The Thorpe Ancestral Trail|Thorpe and Lemmon]]** lines moved through the river valleys of Ohio, while the **[[Topics/The North-West Path|Prior and Spicer]]** lines wandered through the wilderness of Wisconsin and Minnesota. 

In the mid-20th century, these deep histories finally converged. The marriage of **Karen Lu Spicer** and **Robert Archer John "Butch" Thorpe** united the legacies of the "Pioneer Farmer" and the "Civil War Hero" into a single family story.

## A Generational Gift

The reason this vault exists today is because of the dedication of **Robert "Butch" Thorpe**. Starting in the late 20th century and culminating in his major 2009 census project, Butch painstakingly documented every lead, visited cemeteries, and cross-referenced thousands of records. 

His goal was simple but profound: to preserve the identity of the family for his children, **Lancelot Aubrey Duncan Thorpe** and **Wendolyn Lorelei Thorpe**. He wanted to ensure that the stories of ancestors like Watson Moses Risden and Miller Mathias Tallman would never be forgotten.

## The Inheritors

Today, that legacy continues. Through Wendolyn and her husband **Zach Copley**, the family story has reached a new generation: **Wyatt Alexander Thorpe Copley** and **August Quinn Thorpe Copley**. 

Wyatt and August are the inheritors of a 200-year American journey—from the Shenandoah Valley and the farms of Essex to the modern heartland. Every clickable link and interactive timeline in this digital vault is a modernization of Butch’s original gift, ensuring that the "Living Legacy" remains vibrant and accessible for the next century of family history.
