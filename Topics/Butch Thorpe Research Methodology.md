# Butch Thorpe Research Methodology

The following is an extract from the **Introduction** of Robert "Butch" Thorpe's *Census Summary for the Individual Ancestors of Lancelot and Wendolyn Thorpe*, originally published October 26, 2009.

## Introduction

> This document brings together census information for the ancestors of Lance and Wendy Thorpe for the years 1841 to 1930. These limits
were selected because 1841 was the first year that all family members were enumerated and therefore, was the first year that a family
could accurately be identified. And 1930 was the last year that information was available at the time of compilation. Some individuals
seem to have defied enumeration. Perhaps the census taker missed them or perhaps their names were spelled an unusual, unlocatable
way. Whatever, the list is as complete as I can make it and will be added to as new information becomes available. Index entries only list direct ancestors. Some census information for brothers and sisters of ancestors has been collected but has not been indexed.

The Adobe Acrobat versions of this document support hyperlinks of Contents and Index entries. Click on a contents entry or a name in the index and the document will jump to the correct page. Index links will not work on cross-references that say, “See such-and-such”.

— *Robert A. J. Thorpe, October 2009*


## Research and Archiving Workflow

Robert "Butch" Thorpe utilized a rigorous archiving workflow supported by **The Master Genealogist (TMG)** software. In his digital diagrams and physical archives, he used standardized "badges" to indicate the status of sources and citations.

### TMG Software Badges

These icons appear frequently in original exports and signify important research milestones:

| Badge | Meaning |
| :---: | :--- |
| ![[References/raw/extracted/images/burials/burial-000.png]] | **Source Data Distributed**: Indicates source information has been reconciled and propagated through the TMG database. |
| ![[References/raw/extracted/images/burials/burial-001.png]] | **Source Added and Cited**: Confirms a specific primary source has been officially added to the individual's record and cited properly. |

By documenting these badges, we preserve the metadata behind the "RIP", "Obit", and color-coded dots seen in the pedigree charts.
