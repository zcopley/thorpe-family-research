# UK Parish and Regional Context

## Overview

The Thorpe family research vault captures ancestors documented in UK parishes during the 1841–1871 census era, primarily concentrated in the East Midlands and East Anglia regions. This page provides geographic context for understanding settlement patterns and census documentation.

## Primary Parishes and Regions

### Lincolnshire (East Midlands)
**Key Families:** Bellamy, Kelly, Emblow, Sorrell, Thorogood

- **Bourn** — Center of Bellamy family documentation (1841–1871)
  - [[People/James Bellamy|James Bellamy]] (1787–c.1867) — Miller, later Bedehouse Man
  - [[People/Richard Bellamy|Richard Bellamy]] — Agricultural laborer
  - [[People/Henry James Bellamy|Henry James Bellamy]] — Related line
- **Lincolnshire counties documentation:** HO107 (1841), RG9 (1861), RG10 (1871) series
- **Migration pattern:** Rural Lincolnshire to urban Bedhouses in later life (economic transition)

### Related Regions (Implied from Census References)

**Somerset/Devon** — Sorrell and Thorogood surname distribution suggests West Country origin
- Agricultural communities in 1841–1871 census era
- Family names scattered across parishes but not yet reconciled to specific locations

**County Tyrone, Ireland / Massachusetts** — McIntyre compiled-genealogy origin trail
- [[People/William McIntyre|William McIntyre]] (1754–after 1789) documented in the MacIntyre book outprint as father of [[People/Major McIntyre|Major McIntyre]]
- The same outprint traces an older compiled line to John McIntyre of Kilmoore, County Tyrone, Ireland, in Massachusetts before 1719
- US settlement continued into Ohio and Wisconsin in later generations
