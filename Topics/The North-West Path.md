# The North-West Path: Through Wisconsin and Minnesota

While other branches of the family moved directly into Iowa from Ohio, the Prior, Palmer, and Lewis lines took a different route. Their story is one of a "North-West Path," tracing a migration through the developing territories of **Wisconsin** and **Minnesota** before finally settling in the Iowa heartland.

## The Wandering Lewises

The story begins with the Lewis line, led by patriarchs like **[[People/Wynat Lewis|Wynant Williamson Lewis]]**. Unlike the settled farmers of Ohio, the Lewises were often on the move. Records show them appearing in **Massachusetts** and **Vermont** before making the leap into **Wisconsin** by the mid-1800s. 

This migration was part of a larger movement of New Englanders looking for fertile land in the North-West Territory. By the 1850s and 60s, the Lewises had established a presence in **Fond du Lac, Wisconsin**, where they began to intermarry with other pioneering families.

## The Palmer Connection

During this same period, the Palmer family was following a similar trajectory. **[[People/John K Palmer|John K. Palmer]]** moved his household through **Minnesota** in the late 1800s. The Palmers were part of the generation that saw the expansion of the American North-West, adapting to the harsh winters and frontier conditions of the upper Midwest.

## The Prior Integration

The **Prior** line eventually linked these two "wandering" families together. **[[People/Arthur Edwin Prior|Arthur Edwin Prior]]** and **[[People/Martha Eliza Lewis|Martha Eliza Lewis]]** united the Prior and Lewis histories, while the next generation—**[[People/Oliver Warren Prior|Oliver Warren Prior]]**—would marry **[[People/May Aleen Palmer|May Aleen Palmer]]**, bringing the Palmer line into the fold.

## Destination: Iowa

By the early 20th century, the North-West Path finally led to **Iowa**. The descendants of these Wisconsin and Minnesota pioneers established themselves in counties like **Linn and Benton**, joining the other branches of the family that had arrived via the Ohio route. 

The story of the "North-West Path" highlights the diversity of the Thorpe family's origins—not everyone came from the same place, but all eventually found their way to the shared future in Iowa.
