# Visual Legend for Pedigree Timelines

This project utilizes a sophisticated notation system developed by Robert "Butch" Thorpe in his original CorelDRAW pedigree diagrams. These symbols provide high-signal indicators of available records and research status.

## Symbols and Indicators

### Lifespan Bars
![[assets/snippets/Ralph Gerald Thorpe.svg]]
- **Horizontal Bar**: Represents the lifespan of an ancestor.
- **Scale**: The timeline at the top/bottom of the diagram indicates years from 1700 to 2000+.

### Record Indicators (Dots)
- <span style="color: #e30016; font-size: 1.5em;">●</span> **Solid Red Circle**: Indicates a Primary Record or Certificate is held in the physical archive. Often accompanied by a 3-digit reference number (e.g., `#052`).
- <span style="color: #e30016; font-size: 1.5em;">○</span> **Hollow Red Circle**: Indicates a possible certificate or record that has been identified but not yet fully verified or obtained.
- <span style="color: #009ee0; font-size: 1.5em;">●</span> **Solid Blue Circle**: Indicates the individual was found in the Census for that specific year (aligned vertically with the timeline).
- <span style="color: #009ee0; font-size: 1.5em;">○</span> **Hollow Blue Circle**: Indicates a possible census match that requires further verification.

### Status Icons
- **RIP Marker**: Burial site has been determined and/or visited.
- **Obit Marker**: A physical or digital copy of the individual's obituary is available.

## How to Use This Information
The "Source Indicators" section on each person's page translates these visual symbols into text leads. For example:
- **Census Records: Found in 1850, 1860**
- **Official Records: Ref #052**

These indicators point toward the existence of primary sources that can be used to verify the biographical data presented in this wiki.
