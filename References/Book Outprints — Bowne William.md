# William Bowne of Yorkshire, England and His Descendants

## Publication Details

- **BOOK Number:** 019
- **Family/Subject:** Bowne family and Monmouth, New Jersey, Lincoln-family context
- **Source Collection:** [[References/Book Outprints Collection|Book Outprints Collection]]
- **Printed title:** *William Bowne of Yorkshire, England and His Descendants*

## Description

Compiled Bowne family history that covers early Salem, Gravesend, and Middletown settlement history, then turns to a Monmouth County Lincoln-family section.

## Scope & Content

This 7-page outprint was reviewed from the Fairfield County intake. `wbowne.pdf` extracted cleanly with `pdftotext`, and `bowne.doc` is an RTF duplicate of the same material.

## Extracted Evidence from Reviewed Pages

- Pages 1-2: title page and the opening Bowne family narrative.
- Pages 2-4: William Bowne and Ann his wife emigrated from Yorkshire, settled at Salem in 1631, and later moved through Gravesend to Middletown.
- Page 2: William Bowne was granted forty acres at Jeffries Creek in 1636; he was one of the magistrates of Gravesend in 1657 and died at Portland Point in 1677.
- Page 3: William Bowne was the first associate chosen at the Portland Point meeting; John Bowne is described as an elder son and a founder of the Baptist church at Middletown.
- Page 3: the children of William and Ann Bowne are listed as John, James, Andrew, Philip, and daughters.
- Page 4: Captain Andrew Bowne is described as son of William and Ann Bowne, with later colonial offices.
- Page 5: the Lincoln section notes Hannah Lincoln in the 1714 will of Captain John Bowne 2nd, a 1716 John Salter letter mentioning "my brother Lincoln," and a statement that Mordecai Lincoln married Hannah, daughter of Richard and Sarah Bowne Salter.
- Page 5: the same section states that Abraham Lincoln of Monmouth later removed to Pennsylvania and that George Boone, named as a trustee in Mordecai Lincoln's will, was Daniel Boone's grandfather.
- Pages 5-6: later Bowne generations include William Bowne (James1, William1), Rebecca Grover, and the family line into James Bowne.
- Page 7: index only.
