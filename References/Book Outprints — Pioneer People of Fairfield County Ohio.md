# Pioneer People of Fairfield County Ohio

## Source

- **Publication:** *Pioneer Period and Pioneer People of Fairfield County, Ohio*
- **Shared Intake folder:** `/mnt/g/My Drive/Thorpe Shared Intake/Book Outprints/Fairfield County outprint/BOOK 00029 Pioneer People of Fairfield County Ohio/`
- **Pages reviewed:** title page and pages 281-283, 315-317
- **Extraction note:** Local OCR tools were unavailable, so these pages were manually reviewed from GIF page images.

## Extracted Evidence

- Pages 281-283 contain a Tallman sketch for [[People/Sarah Wells Tallman|Sarah Wells Tallman]] and [[People/Samuel Tallman|Samuel Tallman]].
- Page 281 states that Sarah Wells married Samuel Tallman at Wellsburg, Virginia, on 28 March 1801, and that the couple moved from Wellsburg to Fairfield County, Ohio, in 1804.
- Page 282 describes Samuel Tallman as a carpenter and cabinetmaker who built many log houses in Greenfield Township and died at the early age of 50.
- Page 282 says Samuel Tallman's father and grandfather, both named William Tallman, moved from Pennsylvania/Rhode Island to Virginia during the American Revolution; it also states that Samuel's mother, [[People/Dinah Boone|Dinah Boone]], was a cousin of Daniel Boone.
- Page 282 states that [[People/Benjamin Tallman 1745-1820|Benjamin Tallman]] and [[People/Dinah Boone|Dinah Boone]] settled at Canal Winchester, Ohio.
- Page 283 names children of Samuel and Sarah Wells Tallman: William, Susannah Harrison, Amrah Scothern, and Phebe Hooker. It also describes Sarah's difficult trip to Ohio with her brother-in-law William Tallman.
- Pages 315-317 discuss the Hooker/Tallman connection. Page 316 states that Richard Hooker married Nancy Tallman on 6 January 1806, names Nancy as a daughter of William Tallman, and says William donated land for the East Ringgold cemetery.

## Research Value

This source gives narrative context for the older Tallman migration into Fairfield County, Ohio. It does not by itself prove the entire Tallman chain down to [[People/Miller Mathias Tallman|Miller Mathias Tallman]], but it helps explain the Ohio settlement context behind the Tallman branch already documented by census, burial, pedigree, and Miller genealogy sources.

## Uncertainties

- The older Tallman relationships are compiled narrative evidence and need primary-source checks in land, probate, cemetery, or church records.
- The source links Samuel Tallman to Benjamin and Dinah Boone Tallman by narrative context, but the exact relationship to [[People/John Tallman|John Tallman]] and [[People/Benjamin B Tallman|Benjamin B Tallman]] still requires reconciliation against the pedigree timeline and civil records.
