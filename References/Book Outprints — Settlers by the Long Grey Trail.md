## Extracted Evidence from Reviewed Pages

- Page 312 lists [[People/Nathaniel Harrison|Nathaniel Harrison]] as a child of Capt. Reuben Harrison and first wife Lydia Harrison.
- Page 312 says Nathaniel Harrison died in 1807 and married [[People/Mary Woodley|Mary Woodley]] in 1784.
- Pages 374-375 identify Mary Woodley as a daughter of Jacob Woodley and Grace Looker.
- Page 375 says Nathaniel Harrison died testate in 1807 and left children named Lydia, Phebe, Gracy, Anna, [[People/Elizabeth Harrison|Elizabeth]], Mary, Jerusha, Edith, Lucretia, Samuel, and Nathaniel.
- Page 375 says litigation over Nathaniel Harrison's heirs identified Elizabeth as wife of [[People/John Tallman|John Tallman]].
- Page 375 says a 10 March 1817 deed included John Tallman and wife Elizabeth, of Fairfield County, Ohio, conveying their share of Nathaniel Harrison land.
- Page 376 lists Elizabeth among Nathaniel and Mary Harrison's children, gives her marriage as 1808 to John Tallman, and says she removed to Ohio.

## Research Value

- Provides compiled-book support for the [[People/Elizabeth Harrison|Elizabeth Harrison]] and [[People/John Tallman|John Tallman]] marriage.
- Adds a Harrison/Woodley parentage trail for Elizabeth Harrison through [[People/Nathaniel Harrison|Nathaniel Harrison]] and [[People/Mary Woodley|Mary Woodley]].
- Gives a specific primary-record target: the 1817 deed involving John Tallman and wife Elizabeth, of Fairfield County, Ohio.
- Helps explain why Rockingham County, Virginia records matter for the older Fairfield County, Ohio Tallman line.
