## High-Value Evidence Extracted

- William Bowne and Ann his wife emigrated from Yorkshire, settled at Salem in 1631, and later moved through Gravesend to Middletown.
- William Bowne was granted forty acres at Jeffries Creek in 1636 and was one of the magistrates of Gravesend in 1657.
- John Bowne is described as an elder son, a Monmouth settler, and a founder of the Baptist church at Middletown.
- The page 5 Lincoln section ties Hannah Lincoln, John Salter, Mordecai Lincoln, and the Bowne/Salter families into the Monmouth record.
- Later pages continue the Bowne descendants through William Bowne (James1, William1) and James Bowne.
