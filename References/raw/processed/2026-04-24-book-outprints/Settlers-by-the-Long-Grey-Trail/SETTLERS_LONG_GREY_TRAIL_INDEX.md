## High-Value Evidence Extracted

- Page 312 places [[People/Nathaniel Harrison|Nathaniel Harrison]] among the children of Capt. Reuben Harrison and first wife Lydia Harrison, and gives Nathaniel as deceased in 1807 and married in 1784 to [[People/Mary Woodley|Mary Woodley]].
- Pages 374-375 identify [[People/Mary Woodley|Mary Woodley]] as a daughter of Jacob Woodley and Grace Looker, and say Mary married [[People/Nathaniel Harrison|Nathaniel Harrison]].
- Page 375 says Nathaniel Harrison died testate in 1807 and left children named Lydia, Phebe, Gracy, Anna, Elizabeth, Mary, Jerusha, Edith, Lucretia, Samuel, and Nathaniel.
- Page 375 says the related Burkholder litigation identifies [[People/Elizabeth Harrison|Elizabeth Harrison]] as wife of [[People/John Tallman|John Tallman]].
- Page 376 lists [[People/Elizabeth Harrison|Elizabeth Harrison]] among Nathaniel and Mary's children, gives her marriage as 1808 to [[People/John Tallman|John Tallman]], and says she removed to Ohio.
- Page 375 states a 10 March 1817 deed involved John Tallman and wife Elizabeth, of Fairfield County, Ohio, conveying their share of Nathaniel Harrison land.
