# Sandusky-Beers — John McIntyre Lemmon

## Source

- **Publication:** *Commemorative Biographical Record of the Counties of Sandusky and Ottawa, Ohio*
- **Publisher:** J. H. Beers & Co., Chicago
- **Year:** 1896

## Extracted Evidence

- Page 607 begins a biographical sketch for [[People/John McIntyre Lemmon|John McIntyre Lemmon]] and describes him as a citizen, soldier, bar member, and judge.
- Page 607 states that John McIntyre Lemmon was born 25 July 1839 in Townsend, Sandusky County, Ohio, son of [[People/Uriah Blake Lemmon|Uriah Blake Lemmon]] and [[People/Emily Amanda MacIntyre|Emily Amanda MacIntyre]] Lemmon.
- Pages 607-608 trace the Lemmon paternal line to Hugh Lemmon, then to James Lemmon Sr., who was born in Northumberland County, Pennsylvania, on 17 July 1779; married [[People/Rebecca Blake|Rebecca Blake]] in 1805; volunteered in the War of 1812; moved to Ohio in 1827; and died at North Ridge, Townsend, Sandusky County, Ohio, on 7 May 1854.
- Page 608 states that [[People/Uriah Blake Lemmon|Uriah Blake Lemmon]] died at Clyde, Ohio, on 16 February 1887, and that his wife died 12 July 1860 in Townsend Township, Sandusky County.
- Pages 608-610 summarize John McIntyre Lemmon's education, law study, Civil War service, promotion, marriage, public office, and death.
- Page 610 states that John McIntyre Lemmon married Annie Covell on 29 March 1864 at Perkins, Erie County, Ohio; was elected first mayor of Clyde on 11 June 1866; was appointed judge of the common pleas court in October 1866; toured France, England, Scotland, and Wales in summer 1887; and died at his home in Clyde on 17 August 1895.

## Research Value

This source is a compiled county biography, not a vital record. It is nevertheless valuable because it directly identifies the parent-child relationship between John McIntyre Lemmon, [[People/Uriah Blake Lemmon|Uriah Blake Lemmon]], and [[People/Emily Amanda MacIntyre|Emily Amanda MacIntyre]], and it clarifies that [[People/James Lemmon|James Lemmon]] and [[People/Rebecca Blake|Rebecca Blake]] are John McIntyre Lemmon's grandparents.
