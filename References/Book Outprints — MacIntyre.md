## Extracted Evidence from Reviewed Pages

- Page 161 identifies [[People/John McIntyre of Kilmoore|John McIntyre of Kilmoore]] as born in the parish of Kilmoore, County Tyrone, Ireland, about 1678; as having come from Ireland before 1719; as having died at Rutland, Massachusetts, on 26 May 1759; and as married to Grace, who survived him.
- Pages 161-162 list children of John and Grace McIntyre, including Agnes, Janet, Elizabeth, Martha, Mary, Christian, John, James, and Thomas.
- Page 163 says the younger John McIntyre, child of John and Grace, served in Massachusetts companies during the American Revolution, including service connected with the 19 April 1775 alarm, the 1776 march to Mount Independence, the 1777 Bennington alarm, and a 1782 Northampton alarm.
- Pages 163-164 identify [[People/William McIntyre|William McIntyre]] as probably a son of Thomas and Sarah McIntyre, born 1 March 1754, married at Chester, Massachusetts, on 25 July 1778 to Rosanna Campbell, and recorded in the 1790 census at Plantation 7, Hampshire County, Massachusetts.
- Page 164 says William McIntyre served in Captain Nathan Rowlee's company at Ticonderoga from 25 December 1776 to 3 April 1777, enlisted 9 July 1777, served 39 days on a march to reinforce the Northern Army, served seven days in August 1777 with Captain Enoch Shepard's company on a Bennington alarm, and served one month from 21 September 1777 on a Saratoga expedition.
- Page 164 lists William and Rosanna Campbell McIntyre's children as Betsey, Thomas, William, Zimri, Anna, [[People/Major McIntyre|Major]], and Ananias, with Major McIntyre born 3 May 1789.
- Page 165 identifies Alexander McIntyre, son of Alexander and Elizabeth Robinson McIntyre, as a physician and surgeon in Palmyra, New York, and lists his son Samuel Beckwith McIntyre as a Palmyra lawyer, Civil War officer, and GAR member.
- Page 165 also records an unrelated or not-yet-connected James McIntire, born in Ireland about 1838, who enlisted in the 12th New Hampshire Regiment during the Civil War and was wounded in 1864.

## Research Value
