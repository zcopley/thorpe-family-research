## Extracted Evidence from Reviewed Pages

- Page 11 says [[People/Samuel Miller|Samuel Miller]] was probably born in York County, Pennsylvania, about 1738, but that no definite birth record had been found.
- Page 11 says Samuel Miller married Magdalena Wiley about 1758 or 1759; the author says no record was found for Samuel's birth or marriage and presents this as a deduction.
- Pages 11-12 say Samuel Miller was in York County tax records in 1777 and purchased land in Rockingham County, Virginia, in 1782, about two miles west of Harrisonburg.
- Page 12 says a 1785 transfer from Nehemiah Harrison to Samuel Miller was found and that Samuel's land holdings included 215 acres at purchase and more acreage by the time of his death.
- Page 13 says Samuel Miller probably died in 1789 and lists known children: David, Elizabeth, Christly, Michael, Abraham, Barbara, [[People/John Miller|John]], Anna, Samuel, and Magdalena.
- Pages 14-15 summarize the children of Samuel Miller, including the statement that John Miller entered land west of the Eversole schoolhouse between Thurston and Millersport, Ohio.
- Page 15 identifies [[People/John Miller|John Miller]] as born in York County, Pennsylvania; moving with his parents to Rockingham County, Virginia, about 1782; marrying Anna Smootz; and moving to Fairfield County, Ohio, in 1805.
- Page 15 says John Miller settled about two miles south of Millersport, Ohio, on the farm now owned by Lewis Hite.
- Page 240 identifies [[People/Mathias Miller|Mathias Miller]] as born in Rockingham County, Virginia; moved with his parents to Fairfield County, Ohio, about 1805; married Amy E. Critton about 1818; owned land around Millersport; was a founder of the town; and was a contractor in digging the Ohio Canal.
- Page 240 gives Mathias Miller as born 7 Jan 1789 and died 4 May 1871.
- Pages 240-241 identify [[People/Romancy Miller|Romancy Miller]] as a child of Mathias Miller and Amy E. Critton, married to Benjamin F. Tallman on 25 Oct 1834 at Millersport, Ohio, with moves to Canal Winchester about 1845, Jones County, Iowa, in 1848, and later Rippey, Greene County, Iowa.
- Pages 241-242 identify [[People/Miller Mathias Tallman|Miller Mathias Tallman]] as a child of Romancy Miller and Benjamin Tallman, born 14 Apr 1841.
- Page 242 says Mathias M. Tallman married Lizzie Wimmer on 24 Aug 1872 and lists children William Gilmore Tallman and Elvin Lenora Tallman.
- Page 242 also has a lower summary headed "Children of Mathias M. Tallman" that gives the children as Willis G. Tallman and Lenore Tallman, matching the names used in census-derived vault summaries more closely than the preceding William Gilmore/Elvin Lenora wording.
- Page 250 says Wm. Gilmore Tallman married Edith A. Dean on 10 June 1897 and had Richard D. Tallman and Elvin Lenora Tallman.
- Page 250 says Elvin Lenora Tallman married U. B. Thorpe on 22 October 1903, gives U. B. Thorpe as born 14 July 1878, and lists a child Ralph Thorpe born 27 October 1906.
