## Extracted Evidence

- The Iowa Grand Army of the Republic overview lists Watson M. Risden among Iowa Department commanders for the June 1928 to June 1933 span.
- The biographical page in `Grand-Army-Risden.txt` states that [[People/Watson Moses Risden|Watson Moses Risden]] was born in Watertown, New York, on 1 December 1843 and moved westward to Indiana in youth.
- The same page states that he enlisted in spring 1864 in the 128th Indiana Volunteer Infantry, served at Dalton, Rocky Face, Buzzard's Roost, Franklin, Nashville, and Kingston, and was mustered out in April 1866.
- After the war, the page says he moved directly to Iowa and lived in Cedar Rapids for more than fifty-five years.
- The page identifies him as active in local Post No. 235 at Cedar Rapids and in the Iowa Department of the Grand Army of the Republic.
- It states he was elected Iowa Department Commander at the 1930 encampment, then elected Department Chaplain in 1931 and 1932, and died on 23 June 1932 shortly after reelection.

## Research Value

This source materially strengthens the military and civic-service context for Watson Moses Risden. It also introduces a death-date discrepancy: the Grand Army page gives 23 June 1932, while the burial and census-summary material currently used in the vault gives 30 June 1932.

## Uncertainties

- The Grand Army source is a web-capture/compiled memorial page, not a death certificate.
- The date conflict should be resolved against cemetery, obituary, probate, or state death records before changing the canonical death date.
