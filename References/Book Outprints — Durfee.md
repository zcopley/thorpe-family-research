## Extracted Evidence from Reviewed Pages

- Volume I page 31 says [[People/Patience Durfee Tallman|Patience Durfee]] was a daughter of Thomas Durfee and Deliverance Hall/Tripp Durfee, died at Portsmouth, Rhode Island, in 1723, and married [[People/Benjamin Tallman 1684-1769|Benjamin Tallman]] on 23 September 1708.
- Volume I page 31 says Benjamin Tallman was a son of Peter Tallman and Joan Briggs Tallman, was born in Portsmouth, Rhode Island, on 28 January 1684, and died at Warwick, Rhode Island, on 20 May 1769.
- Volume II page 571 says [[People/William Tallman 1720-1791|William Tallman]] was the fourth child of Benjamin and Patience Durfee Tallman, born at Portsmouth, Rhode Island, on 25 March 1720, and died near Harrisonburg, Virginia, on 13 February 1791.
- Volume II page 571 says William Tallman married [[People/Ann Lincoln Tallman|Ann Lincoln]] in Berks County, Pennsylvania, on 20 October 1740 or 1742.
- Volume II page 571 identifies Ann Lincoln as a daughter of Mordecai Lincoln, gives her probable birth in Chester County, Pennsylvania, on 8 March 1725, says she died near Harrisonburg, Virginia, on 22 December about 1812, and says she was buried beside William.
- Volume II page 572 says [[People/Benjamin Tallman 1745-1820|Benjamin Tallman]] was the only child of William and Ann Tallman who survived his parents and married.
- Volume II page 572 says Benjamin Tallman was born in Berks County, Pennsylvania, on 9 January 1745, died near Canal Winchester, Ohio, on 4 June 1820, married [[People/Dinah Boone|Dinah Boone]] in Berks County on 9 November 1764, removed to Virginia in 1779, served in Pennsylvania militia and later in Virginia, and removed to Ohio around 1810.
- Volume II page 572 says Dinah Boone was born in Berks County, Pennsylvania, on 3 or 10 May 1749, baptized at Morlatton Church on 6 August 1753, died near Canal Winchester, Ohio, on 25 July 1824, and was a first cousin of Daniel Boone.

## Research Value

- Extends the older Tallman branch behind [[People/Benjamin Tallman 1745-1820|Benjamin Tallman 1745-1820]] through [[People/William Tallman 1720-1791|William Tallman]] and [[People/Ann Lincoln Tallman|Ann Lincoln Tallman]].
- Connects the Tallman branch to the earlier Rhode Island Tallman/Durfee generation through [[People/Benjamin Tallman 1684-1769|Benjamin Tallman 1684-1769]] and [[People/Patience Durfee Tallman|Patience Durfee Tallman]].
- Adds migration context: Rhode Island to Berks County, Pennsylvania, to Rockingham County, Virginia, to Canal Winchester, Ohio.
- Provides specific follow-up targets: William Tallman's Bible record, Pennsylvania militia records, Virginia bounty-land records, and Morlatton Church baptism records.
