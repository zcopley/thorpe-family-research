### Lewis Family Vermont to Minnesota Settlement Arc

**Scope:** 140-year multi-generational journey (1782–1923)

**Key Individuals:**
- Wynat Lewis (patriarch, b. ~1782, Vermont-born farmer, age 68 at Wisconsin 1850)
- Susan Lewis (wife, b. ~1796, married Wynat c.1800)
- Oliver Warren Lewis (son, b. 1823–1892, Vermont-born farmer at Wisconsin 1850 age 25, continued Minnesota settlement age 57 at Mower County 1880)
- Martha Eliza Lewis (granddaughter, b. 1859 Fond du Lac County Wisconsin, continued Minnesota/South Dakota/Minnesota settlement 1880–1923, age 64 at 1923)

**Generation Arc:**
- Generation 1: Wynat Lewis Vermont patriarch (1782–1850s) — 68+ year Vermont farming, then age-68 relocation to Wisconsin 1850
- Generation 1 Continued: Wisconsin intermediate settlement (Racine County 1850, Fond du Lac County 1860) — 30-year Wisconsin residence, family expansion with Martha E. Lewis born Wisconsin 1859
- Generation 2: Oliver Warren Lewis continuation (Wisconsin 1850–1880, Minnesota 1880–1892) — documented farmer continuity across three states, successful Mower County Minnesota settlement
- Generation 3: Martha Eliza Lewis (Wisconsin-born 1859, Minnesota/South Dakota 1880–1923) — granddaughter continuing family settlement pattern into early 20th century

**Narrative Structure:**
- Vermont Origins section (1782–1850): family farming establishment, documented children, economic status
- Racine County Wisconsin Entry (1850): age-68 relocation, 8-member household, farming continuity
- Fond du Lac County Wisconsin Continuation (1860): 10-year interval, geographic shift from rural to urban Ripon Ward, Martha E. Lewis birth 1859
- Mower County Minnesota Settlement (1880–1923): final settlement, Oliver Warren Lewis age 57 farmer, Arthur Prior/Martha Lewis household documentation, 43-year settlement span

**Data Elements:**

- Detailed household composition (size, ages, occupations, birthplaces)

- Settlement timeline table (Vermont origins, Racine County entry, Fond du Lac continuation, Mower County final)

- Economic indicators (household size correlations, property value trends, family labor availability)

**Research Applications:**

- For economic historians: frontier agricultural settlement viability, occupational continuity across regions, family labor economics
