# Uriah Blake Thorpe

## Biographical Profile

- **Name:** Uriah Blake Thorpe
- **Role in this project:** Thorpe-line ancestor listed in the census-summary index.

## Source-Cited Facts

- A census-summary entry gives Uriah Blake Thorpe as born 14 Jul 1878 and died 19 May 1959.
- The Thorpe pedigree timeline includes `Uriah Blake Thorpe 1878-1959` on the compiled Thorpe family branch chart.
- The Thorpe pedigree timeline places Uriah Blake Thorpe below William Monroe Thorp and above Raymond Miller Thorpe in the direct line.
- The Burial Sites book places Uriah Blake Thorpe at Evergreen Cemetery in Grand Mound, Iowa (page 44), Block ____, Lot ____, with date of death 19 May 1959 and inscription `THORPE / FATHER / U. B. / 1878 – 1959`. Map: [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Evergreen+Cemetery+Grand+Mound+IA).
- The *Genealogy and History of Samuel Miller* page 250 says Elvin Lenora Tallman married U. B. Thorpe on 22 October 1903, gives U. B. Thorpe as born 14 July 1878, and lists a child Ralph Thorpe born 27 October 1906.

## Family Connections

- **Spouse:** [[People/Lenore Hetty Tallman|Lenore Hetty Tallman]], named as Elvin Lenora Tallman in the Miller genealogy and as Lenore Hetty Tallman in the Thorpe pedigree timeline.
- **Child:** Ralph Thorpe, born 27 October 1906 in the Miller genealogy; census summaries identify him as Ralph/Ralph G. Thorpe in the 1910-1930 U. B. Thorpe household.
- **Child:** [[People/Raymond Miller Thorpe|Raymond Miller Thorpe]], based on the Thorpe pedigree timeline and census summaries.

## Identity Note

**Separate from [[People/Uriah Blake Lemmon|Uriah Blake Lemmon]]** (1808-1887, Ohio). These are different individuals:
- Birth year difference: 70 years (1878 vs 1808)
- Different locations: Iowa vs Ohio  
- Death dates: 1959 vs 1887
- Different burial sites: Evergreen Cemetery, Grand Mound vs McPherson Cemetery, Clyde
- Likely related through Blake family or Lemmon/Thorpe connection based on pedigree timeline grouping, but definitely different generations.
