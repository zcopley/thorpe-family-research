# Watson Moses Risden

## Biographical Profile

- **Name:** Watson Moses Risden
- **Role in this project:** Risden-line household head represented in 1860-1930 census-summary chains.

## Source-Cited Facts

- A census-summary entry gives Watson Moses Risden as born 1 Dec 1843 and died 30 Jun 1932.
- The profile includes 1860 Lyons Township, Iowa; 1870 Lapeer, Michigan; 1880 Cedar Rapids, Iowa; 1900 Cedar Rapids, Iowa; 1920 Benton County, Iowa; and 1930 Cedar Rapids, Iowa entries.
- The extracted chain places Watson with spouse Nancy and later as father in Anderson household entries.
- The Burial Sites book places Watson Moses Risden at Linwood Cemetery in Cedar Rapids, Iowa (page 29), Block 3, 3rd Addition, S½ Lot 135 - Burial 6441, with GPS coordinates `41°57’36.3”N 91°40’25.1”W` and date of death 30 June 1932. Map: [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Linwood+Cemetery+Cedar+Rapids+IA).
- The Grand Army records state that Watson Moses Risden was born in Watertown, New York, on 1 Dec 1843; moved westward to Indiana in youth; enlisted in spring 1864 in the 128th Indiana Volunteer Infantry; served at Dalton, Rocky Face, Buzzard's Roost, Franklin, Nashville, and Kingston; and was mustered out in April 1866.
- The same Grand Army biographical page says he came directly to Iowa after the war, lived in Cedar Rapids for more than fifty-five years, was active in G.A.R. Post No. 235 at Cedar Rapids, was elected Iowa Department Commander in 1930, and served as Department Chaplain in 1931 and 1932.
- The processed Spicer timeline review keeps Watson in the Risden direct line between `John Wheeler Risden` and `Hattie May Risden`.

## Date Discrepancy

- Burial and census-summary material currently give Watson Moses Risden's death as 30 June 1932.
- The Grand Army biographical page says he died on 23 June 1932, shortly after reelection as Department Chaplain.
- Keep the canonical date unresolved until checked against cemetery, obituary, probate, or state death records.
