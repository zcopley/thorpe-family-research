# Lester Harold Spicer

## Biographical Profile

- **Name:** Lester Harold Spicer
- **Birth/Death:** Born 14 Jul 1906; died 28 Jun 1974 (age 67 years, 11 months, 14 days)
- **Role in this project:** Third-generation Spicer; son of George B. Spicer (farmer); transition from agricultural to industrial occupation (car repairer); married [[People/Ruby Bernice Prior|Ruby Bernice Prior]].
- **Burial:** Cedar Memorial Cemetery, Cedar Rapids, Iowa; `64K Last Supper, Space 4`; GPS 42°1'24.2"N 91°38'17.4"W; inscription `LESTER H. / 1906 † 1974`; Burial Sites book, page 33.

## Census Records and Life Progression

- **Head:** `George Spicer`, male, age 45, farmer
- **Mother:** `Hattie Spicer`, female, age 32
- **Lester H. Spicer** appears as son, male, age 3
- **Siblings:** Edith (9), Charles R. (8), George G. (6), Chester J. (2.3)
- **Uncle:** William Spicer, male, age 49 (farmer laborer)

### 1920 Iowa Census — Benton County, Benton Township (as adolescent)
- **Head:** `George Spicer`, male, age 55, farmer
- **Mother:** `Hattie Spicer`, female, age 42
- **Lester H. Spicer** appears as son, male, age 13
- **Siblings:** Charles R. (17, farm laborer), George J. (15, farm laborer), Chester J. (11), Edna (7), Myron (4+)
- **Note:** Older brothers already working as farm laborers; Lester still in school/home
- **Source:** Series T625, Roll 477, Pages 4B & 5A, ED 4

### 1930 Iowa Census — Linn County, Clinton Township (as young adult)
- **Head:** `George Spicer`, male, age 65, farmer
- **Mother:** `Hattie Spicer`, female, age 53
- **Lester H. Spicer**, male, age 23, **car repairer (railroad shop)** — occupational shift away from agriculture
- **Siblings:** Chester J. (21, farmer), Edna (17), Myron L. (14)
- **Source:** Series T626, Roll 664, Page 11A, ED 8

### 1940 Iowa Census — Linn County, Cedar Rapids, 17th Ward (as adult)
- **Head:** `Lester H. Spicer`, male, age 32, **torch operator, rock crushing manufacturer**
- **Wife:** `Ruby B. Spicer`, female, age 26
- **Son:** Richard Spicer, male, age 4
- **Address:** 724 Seventh Street SW, Cedar Rapids
- **Note:** Established in town with manufacturing job; one child; urban rather than rural residence
- **Source:** Series T9, Roll [unlisted], Page [unlisted], ED [unlisted]

## Occupational Transition

Lester Harold Spicer represents the **agricultural-to-industrial transition** of the 1920s-1940s:
- **1910-1920:** Child of farmer, in agricultural household
- **1920:** Age 13, farming household (brothers working as farm laborers)
- **1930:** Age 23, **occupational pivot** — car repairer for railroad (skilled industrial trade, not farm labor)
- **1940:** Age 32, torch operator in rock crushing factory; established in Cedar Rapids (urban center)
